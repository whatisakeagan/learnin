Tigger's Quirks

My dog's name being Tigger should tell you that she's not exactly typical. Here's a brief catalog of all the ways in which she continues to make be scratch my head.

How She Got Her Name

When I first picked Tigger up and brought her home, her preferred method of locomotion was to literally hop around, as opposed to, you know, walking. Given it looked like she was always bouncing on her back end, "Tigger" seemed to be an appropriate name.

(I realize most people think it should only be a "boy's" dog name, but I don't think dogs could possibly care less about gender.)

Her Babies

Tigger has always had a fascination for her "babies," which are no more than stuffed animals. While she would have previously ripped them apart to see the insides (teething, maybe?) she now insists on cuddling with at least one whenever she's resting. She also brings them along with her wherever she goes.

Cuddling & Sleeping

Tigger is a very needy sleeper--she must be in contact with me in some fashion at practically all times. As you can see to the left, this was much cuter when she was a puppy--now it's cute only some of the time. Sure, I appreciate the constant heating element in the bed, but having 45 pounds laying on my feet all the time can get old. I guess I should just be thankful she only kicks me like a donkey when I've severely disrupte her slumber, and that she likes being around me so much.

Glamour Shots

She can take some adorable photos, no doubt, but she's always had a knack for looking like she's posing for a bad 90s glamourshot. Those teeth, man.

Tennis Balls: More = Better

Like any good black lab, Tigger LOVES tennis balls. She feels incomplete if there are any less than 2 in her mouth, and will actively try to catch a ball if she already has one in her mouth, even though it's never worked, and she always has to corner it to get both in her mouth at the same time. She is so devoted to her tennis ball cause that she rejects the laws of the real world and continues to believe she'll catch that second ball one day.
